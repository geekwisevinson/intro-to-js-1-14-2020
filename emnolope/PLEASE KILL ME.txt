Ok so you got the input right?
Then you got the output right?
So there are networks that take in all sorts of data. Like event camera data, high fps data, high res data, depth camera data, and weirder stuff like foot pressure pad data and like laser shits. There are even things that only work with special annoated forward backwards limb data, and 2d pose data only
Then you got output right? There's two main types 2d output and 3d output. That's pretty simple!

And then there's like frequency of sensors. Some things are high latency, like neural network forward passes, but some things are low latency, like wired IMU components.
If you match the high latency things with low frequency phenoms and the low latency with high frequency phenoms, you can use sensor fusion to get a complete picture of what's happening in real time!

And then there's also like boundary conditions on the network, which can be done with GANS, latent vectors, parametric models, biomechanica l models, etc...
